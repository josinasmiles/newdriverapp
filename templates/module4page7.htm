Records & Safety
CRASHES:
Do not stop at a crash unless you are involved or if emergency help has not yet arrived. Do not block the way for police, firefighters, ambulances, tow trucks and other rescue vehicles.
If you are in a crash, you should do the following:
1. Stop your vehicle at or near the crash scene. If you can, move your vehicle off of the road so you do not block traffic. Many crashes are caused when traffic stops or slows for an initial crash.

2. Call the police, if anyone is hurt or dies. If the drivers of the vehicles are hurt and cannot call the police, then witnesses at or near the crash scene must call for help.

3. Call the police, if any vehicle needs to be towed.
